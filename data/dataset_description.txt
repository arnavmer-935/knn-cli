We provide three datasets for evaluating your solution. A brief overview of each is as follows:  

- Iris dataset: Often considered the “Hello World” of data science and machine learning, this dataset is widely used
to test and demonstrate classification algorithms. It consists of flower measurements used to predict the species
of an iris.

- Palmer Penguins dataset: Designed as a modern alternative to the Iris dataset, this dataset contains body size
measurements for three penguin species.

- Word Vectors dataset: This dataset contains pre-trained word embeddings, numerical representations of words as
high-dimensional vectors. Obtained from GloVe, these embeddings capture both semantic and syntactic relationships,
making them useful for tasks involving word meaning and similarity.

We recommend beginning your project with the Iris dataset to develop and evaluate your implementation. Once complete,
you can test additional functionalities with the other datasets:

- The Penguins dataset is larger and includes more features than the Iris dataset. This makes it a good resource
for running additional test cases, particularly when testing data visualization and descriptive statistics functionalities.

- The Word Vectors dataset is less suitable for visualization or descriptive summaries due to its high dimensionality,
but it serves as a valuable stress test. It contains 50 features and significantly more data points than the other
datasets, making it useful for assessing scalability and performance.
